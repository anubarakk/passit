# 3. What Every Reviewer Looks Like

Same structure every time, so quality is always consistent:

1. **⚡ At a glance** — the whole topic in a few lines
2. **📖 Simple explanations** — hard ideas in easy words
3. **🧠 Memory tricks** — mnemonics and analogies
4. **📊 Tables** — comparisons and key facts, easy to scan
5. **⭐ Study this first** — the most exam-likely items
6. **📝 Quick check** — 2–3 questions to see if it stuck *(want a real test? That's Mock Exam)*

## Sample (mini Quick Reviewer: "Photosynthesis")

**⚡ At a glance:** Photosynthesis = how plants make food. Sunlight + water + CO₂ → sugar + oxygen. Happens in the chloroplast, in two stages.

**📖 Simple explanation:** A plant is like a solar-powered kitchen. The solar panel (chlorophyll) catches sunlight, the kitchen (chloroplast) cooks water and CO₂ into sugar, and oxygen is the leftover.

**🧠 Memory trick:** "**L**ight **C**ooks **W**ater" → **L**ight stage first, then **C**alvin cycle; **W**ater is split in stage 1.

**📊 Key table:**

| Stage | Where | Needs | Makes |
|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| Light stage | Thylakoid | Sunlight, water | Energy (ATP), oxygen |
| Calvin cycle | Stroma | Energy, CO₂ | Sugar |

**⭐ Study this first:** the word equation, the two stages, where each happens.

**📝 Quick check:** 1) What are the two stages? 2) Which gas is released?
